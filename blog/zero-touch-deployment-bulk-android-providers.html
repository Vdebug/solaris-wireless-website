Zero-Touch Deployment for Bulk Android: Provider Comparison

30 April 2026 11 min read
By the Solaris Wireless Procurement Team Last updated: April 30, 2026
Quick answer

Google Zero-Touch Enrollment lets enterprises auto-enrol bulk Android devices into MDM on first activation, requiring zero IT staging. Devices must be sourced from a Zero-Touch registered reseller who registers each device's IMEI to your zero-touch portal before shipment. Solaris Wireless coordinates Zero-Touch enrolment through registered partner relationships.

Key facts
  • What it is: Google's auto-enrolment programme for Android
  • Equivalent to: Apple Business Manager DEP, Samsung Knox Mobile Enrollment
  • Requirement: Source from a Zero-Touch registered reseller
  • Solaris approach: Coordinated through registered partners + direct
  • MDM compatibility: Intune, Workspace ONE, Jamf, MobileIron, SOTI

What Google Zero-Touch Enrollment Solves

Without Zero-Touch, an enterprise deploying 1,000 Android phones to employees has to either (a) ship every device to IT, have IT power on each device, manually enrol each device into the MDM, then re-ship to the employee, or (b) ship to the employee and walk them through the enrolment process via help-desk. Both paths cost real money and slow deployment by weeks.

Zero-Touch Enrollment eliminates this. The reseller registers each device IMEI in the enterprise zero-touch portal before shipment. The employee unboxes the device and powers it on. The device checks in with Google's servers, identifies its owner organisation, and pulls down the MDM enrolment payload from your Zero-Touch portal before the home screen appears. The employee signs in with corporate credentials and the device is fully managed.

Zero-Touch Provider Categories

Three categories of provider participate in the Zero-Touch ecosystem:

1. Carriers

Major carriers (T-Mobile, Verizon, AT&T) are zero-touch registered and can sell devices that ship pre-registered to the enterprise's zero-touch portal. The advantage: integrated billing with the carrier service plan. The trade-off: carrier-locked devices, limited customisation beyond standard MDM enrolment.

2. OEMs and OEM-direct distributors

Samsung, Google (Pixel) and Motorola/Lenovo participate directly through their commercial sales teams. OEM-direct allocation supports volume orders with pre-zero-touch registration. Provisioning depth varies; typically standard MDM enrolment without custom OS or carrier branding.

3. Specialist institutional suppliers

Specialist suppliers like Solaris Wireless coordinate Zero-Touch enrolment alongside deeper provisioning capabilities (custom OS flashing, SIM lock, carrier branding, asset tagging, direct-to-end-user fulfilment). For enterprises that need more than vanilla MDM enrolment, specialist suppliers are typically the right path.

MDM Compatibility for Zero-Touch

Zero-Touch federates with all major Android-supporting MDM platforms: Microsoft Intune, VMware Workspace ONE, Jamf Pro (yes, Jamf supports Android), MobileIron/Ivanti, SOTI MobiControl, Samsung Knox Manage, IBM MaaS360, and most Android Enterprise certified EMM platforms.

The MDM platform must be configured for Zero-Touch on the EMM administrator side: register the EMM with the Zero-Touch portal, configure the enrolment profile, and link to the device assignments. Once configured, every device assigned to the profile auto-enrols on first activation.

Selection Criteria for Zero-Touch Providers

1. Zero-Touch registration status

Confirm the provider is registered in the Google Zero-Touch portal as an authorised reseller. The provider should be able to walk you through their portal access and demonstrate registering a device IMEI in your tenant.

2. OEM relationships

If you need a specific OEM (Samsung Galaxy, Motorola Edge, Pixel, Sonim ruggedised, Kyocera DuraForce), confirm the provider sources from that OEM. Carrier providers and OEM-direct have narrower OEM coverage; specialist suppliers like Solaris Wireless source across the full institutional OEM landscape.

3. Provisioning depth

Decide whether you need vanilla zero-touch (just MDM enrolment) or zero-touch plus custom provisioning (custom OS, SIM lock, carrier branding, asset tagging). Specialist suppliers offer the deeper combination. For most enterprise deployments, vanilla zero-touch is sufficient and any registered provider can deliver it.

4. Direct-to-end-user fulfilment

Confirm the provider can ship direct to your employees rather than to a depot. Direct-to-employee with asset tagging is faster, cheaper and produces a better employee experience than the depot-then-re-ship model.

5. Account management and replacement workflow

100+ device fleets need a named account manager and a defined replacement workflow. Carriers and mass distributors typically offer ticket-based support; specialist suppliers offer dedicated account management with mobile/SMS access.

Common Zero-Touch Mistakes to Avoid

  • Buying from a non-registered reseller and assuming you can register the IMEIs after the fact (you cannot; registration must be pre-shipment by the reseller)
  • Configuring the EMM Zero-Touch profile after device shipment instead of before, leaving devices in limbo when they activate
  • Mismatched OEM coverage between your zero-touch profile and the devices you actually receive (always confirm OEM and model before shipment)
  • Skipping pilot batch testing before scaling the deployment (always validate auto-enrolment on a 5-10 unit test batch first)
  • Mixing zero-touch and non-zero-touch devices in the same deployment without clear labelling, causing confusion at receipt

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I find a Zero-Touch registered Android reseller?

Confirm with the reseller that they are listed in the Google Zero-Touch portal as an authorised reseller. Major carriers (T-Mobile, Verizon, AT&T), OEMs (Samsung, Google, Motorola) and specialist institutional suppliers like Solaris Wireless coordinate Zero-Touch enrolment for enterprise procurement.

What MDM platforms work with Google Zero-Touch?

Most Android Enterprise EMMs: Microsoft Intune, VMware Workspace ONE, Jamf Pro, MobileIron/Ivanti, SOTI MobiControl, Samsung Knox Manage, IBM MaaS360. Configure the EMM with your Zero-Touch portal before deployment.

Can Solaris Wireless ship Zero-Touch enrolled Android phones direct to employees?

Yes. Solaris ships pre-registered devices direct to employees with asset tags. The employee unboxes, powers on, signs in with corporate credentials, and the device auto-enrols to your MDM.

What is the difference between Zero-Touch and Samsung Knox Mobile Enrollment?

Zero-Touch is Google's enrolment programme for any Android device manufacturer (covers Pixel, Motorola, Sonim and many others). Knox Mobile Enrollment is Samsung-specific and offers Samsung-specific configuration depth. Samsung devices can use either; non-Samsung Android devices use Zero-Touch.

Can a device be both SIM-locked to my MVNO and Zero-Touch enrolled?

Yes. Solaris Wireless coordinates SIM lock and custom provisioning alongside Zero-Touch enrolment for MVNO and operator deployments. The combined provisioning lets the device ship subscriber-ready.
